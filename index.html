Overview & Definitions

Abortion – Interruption of pregnancy before viability (usually <20–24 weeks or <500 g). Spontaneous abortion (miscarriage) occurs naturally; induced abortion is intentional.

📊 Incidence
15–30% of recognized pregnancies end in spontaneous abortion. 80% occur in first trimester.
🧬 Common Causes
Chromosomal abnormalities (50–80% of early miscarriages), teratogens, infections, hormonal/uterine anomalies, autoimmune factors.
Key concept: Bleeding in early pregnancy is always abnormal; evaluate for ectopic pregnancy and types of abortion.

Habitual / Recurrent Abortion

  • Definition: Three or more consecutive spontaneous abortions before 20 weeks.
  • Causes: Uterine anomalies (septate, bicornuate), cervical incompetence, chromosomal abnormalities (parental balanced translocation), endocrine disorders (luteal phase defect, thyroid, diabetes), antiphospholipid syndrome, infections, environmental toxins.
  • Diagnostic workup: Parental karyotyping, hysterosalpingography, antiphospholipid antibodies, thyroid function, glucose tolerance, endometrial biopsy.
  • Management: Treat underlying cause: cervical cerclage for incompetence, heparin/aspirin for antiphospholipid syndrome, progesterone supplementation for luteal defect, surgical correction of uterine anomalies.
  • Nursing support: Emotional support, grief counseling, coordination of diagnostic tests, education about future pregnancy monitoring.
Recurrence risk: After three losses, risk of another miscarriage is ~30% without treatment.

Induced Abortion (Elective & Therapeutic)

💊 Medical methods (early ≤10 weeks)
Mifepristone (RU‑486) + misoprostol; methotrexate + misoprostol. Nursing: monitor bleeding, pain, ensure follow‑up for completion.
✂️ Surgical methods
Manual vacuum aspiration (≤12 weeks), dilation and curettage (D&C), dilation and evacuation (D&E; >14 weeks).
⚖️ Therapeutic abortion
Performed to preserve maternal health (e.g., severe cardiac disease, eclampsia, cancer requiring treatment) or due to fetal anomaly (lethal).
⚠️ Illegal/unsafe abortion
High risk of incomplete abortion, sepsis, hemorrhage, uterine perforation, death. Nursing: treat complications, provide nonjudgmental care, offer contraception and counseling.
Post‑abortion care: Monitor vitals, bleeding, pain; RhIG for Rh‑negative; contraceptive counseling; emotional support.

Complications of Abortion

  • Hemorrhage: Excessive blood loss (incomplete or septic abortion). Management: IV fluids, oxytocin, D&C, blood transfusion.
  • Infection (Endometritis, peritonitis): Fever, foul lochia, uterine tenderness. Broad‑spectrum antibiotics, D&C for retained tissue.
  • DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation): Particularly with missed abortion (dead fetus retained >4 weeks). Uncontrolled bleeding, coagulopathy; treat with blood products, evacuation.
  • Uterine perforation: From instrumentation (D&C). Signs: sudden cessation of resistance, abdominal pain, hemorrhage. May require laparotomy.
  • Isoimmunization: Rh‑negative mother exposed to Rh‑positive fetal blood → give RhIG within 72 hours.
  • Psychological sequelae: Grief, depression, guilt, anxiety. Offer counseling and support groups.
🚨 Septic abortion emergency: Fever, tachycardia, hypotension, oliguria → IV antibiotics, D&C, ICU care.

Nursing Care & Grief Support

  • Physical care: Monitor bleeding, vital signs, pain. Administer medications as ordered. Provide perineal care.
  • Rh immune globulin: Give within 72 hours after miscarriage, abortion, or ectopic to Rh‑negative unsensitized women.
  • Emotional support: Acknowledge loss, allow expression of grief, use the term “baby” or “pregnancy” not “products of conception”. Normalize feelings of guilt and sadness.
  • Partner/family: Include partner in support; men may also grieve. Provide resources for counseling.
  • Discharge teaching: Warning signs (fever, heavy bleeding, foul discharge). Activity restrictions for 1–2 weeks. Avoid intercourse/tampons for 2 weeks. Contraception counseling (ovulation can return in 2 weeks).
  • Future pregnancy: Recommend folic acid, wait 1–3 cycles before trying again unless investigating recurrent loss. Reassure that one miscarriage does not increase risk of another.
Key nursing attitude: Nonjudgmental, compassionate, and culturally sensitive care regardless of abortion type.

Mnemonics & Summary Table

Threatened, Inevitable, Complete, Missed, Septic. (Incomplete also important).
⚠️ Causes of Recurrent Loss (U.C.A.N.)
Uterine anomalies, Chromosomal, Antiphospholipid syndrome, Not identified (idiopathic).
🧬 Most Common Cause (1st trimester)
Chromosomal abnormalities (50–80%).
💊 Medical Abortion Drugs
Mifepristone + misoprostol (early). Methotrexate + misoprostol.
🩸 RhIG Indications
Spontaneous or induced abortion, ectopic, molar pregnancy, after 28 weeks also postpartum if infant Rh+.
📋 Nursing Priorities (P.A.C.E.)
Physical support (bleeding, vitals), Assessment (Rh status, emotional), Comfort (pain, grief), Education (discharge, contraception).
